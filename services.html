🌴 Wind Mitigation Inspections

A Wind Mitigation Inspection is one of the most common inspections required by insurance companies when buying or insuring a home in Florida. It identifies your home’s wind-resistant features — like roof shape, coverings, and structural connections — which can help you qualify for insurance discounts or simply give you peace of mind when planning upgrades to better protect your home.

🔎 What’s Included in a Wind Mitigation Inspection

  • Roof Covering Type & Condition – Verifying the material, age, and compliance with current Florida Building Code.
  • Roof Deck Attachment – Inspecting how the roof sheathing is secured to the trusses or rafters.
  • Roof-to-Wall Connections – Checking the hardware (clips, straps, or wraps) that secure the roof to the structure.
  • Roof Geometry – Assessing the shape of the roof (hip, gable, flat) to determine wind resistance.
  • Secondary Water Barrier – Identifying protective layers under the roof covering that help prevent water intrusion.
  • Opening Protection – Inspecting windows, doors, and garage openings for impact-rated glass or storm shutters.
  • Gable End Bracing – Verifying additional reinforcements on gable roofs where applicable.

Four-Point Inspection

A Four-Point Inspection is one of our most requested services and is often required by insurance companies when purchasing or renewing a homeowner’s policy. This focused inspection evaluates the four key systems that determine a home’s insurability — Roof, Electrical, Plumbing, and HVAC — assessing their age, condition, and overall safety.

At Keystone Home Inspections, we specialize in delivering accurate, professional Four-Point Inspections that meet insurance requirements and help our clients secure the coverage they need — quickly, thoroughly, and with confidence.

Pre-Listing Inspections

A Pre-Listing Home Inspection gives sellers and agents a competitive edge — helping you identify potential issues early, make informed repairs, and position your home to attract serious buyers and stronger offers.

By having your home inspected before it hits the market, you gain a clear understanding of its current condition — including any defects, maintenance issues, or major repairs that could impact value or delay closing. This proactive approach allows sellers, agents, and investors to make informed decisions, address concerns early, and present the home with confidence.

Buyers are more likely to trust a property that’s already been professionally inspected, leading to smoother negotiations, faster closings, and stronger offers.

At Keystone Home Inspections, we help you take control of the selling process — giving you the insight you need to sell with confidence and maximize your return.

Specialty Inspections

In addition to our standard home and insurance inspections, Keystone Home Inspections offers a range of specialty add-on services designed to give you a deeper understanding of your property’s condition. These optional inspections can be combined with any full home inspection for a more complete evaluation.

Roof Inspection

A focused inspection performed during and after a new roof installation to help verify proper workmanship, identify concerns early, and provide added peace of mind before final payment is made.

Thermal Imaging

Using advanced infrared technology, we detect temperature variations behind walls, ceilings, and floors — identifying potential issues like moisture intrusion, missing insulation, electrical hotspots, or hidden leaks that are not visible to the naked eye.

Termite (WDO) Inspection

Our termite inspections help uncover signs of wood-destroying organisms (WDOs) such as termites, carpenter ants, and wood rot. Early detection can save thousands in repairs and protect the integrity of your home’s structure.

Irrigation System Inspection

We evaluate your irrigation system for proper function, coverage, and efficiency. This includes checking sprinkler heads, valves, timers, and water pressure to help prevent wasted water and landscape damage.

Pool and Spa Inspection

We perform a detailed review of your pool and spa’s condition, including the structure, decking, safety features, pumps, filters, heaters, and other mechanical components — ensuring everything is operating safely and efficiently.
